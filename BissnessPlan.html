Public Domain Books:

Public domain books are no longer under copyright protection and can be freely used, copied, and distributed by anyone. This means that you can publish public domain books without having to obtain permission from any copyright owners or paying any royalties.

However, it's important to note that not all versions of a book may be in the public domain. For example, a new translation or interpretation of a public domain work can still be protected by copyright if it is considered a new creative work. Therefore, it's important to ensure that the specific version of the book you want to publish is in fact in the public domain before doing so.

Regarding the use of a manuscript scan as an interlinear translation, it would depend on whether the scan itself is in the public domain. If the manuscript is old enough to be in the public domain and the scan is simply a faithful reproduction of the original manuscript, then it would likely be considered in the public domain as well. However, if the scan is a modern reproduction or interpretation of the manuscript, it could be protected by copyright. In general, it's always a good idea to do your research and ensure that the materials you use are in the public domain or that you have obtained the necessary permissions to use them.
